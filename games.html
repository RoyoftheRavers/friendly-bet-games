Sweepstakes

Sweepstakes are fun ‘lottery style’ games where each participant goes into an open draw and gets randomly drawn a team (or horse!) who can win them a prize. No prior knowledge of the sport is required – or even helpful! Sweepstakes offered include the Grand National Sweepstake in horse racing and the FIFA World Cup final tournaments in football.

How to Play

Entry is easy, the organiser chooses the competition, the entry fee and sets the pot prize money distribution breakdown e.g. winner takes all etc. Once you have entered the competition and all players are in the draw will be done. Then it’s time to research your team, or horse, and hope they do the business and win you a prize plus some bragging rights!

Rules - Sweepstakes
• The organiser sets the entry fee (€5, €10, €20 or €50).
• It is possible for the organiser to “enable multiple entries” allowing players to make more than one entry if there is a shortage of players needed.
• Your entry will be confirmed upon receipt of payment.
• Once all players are entered, the draw will take place.
• The organiser determines the prize money distribution, i.e. “winner/first places takes all” before the draw and these can be viewed in each competition.
• The prize money distributed will be 95% of the pot after fees are deducted.

Last Man Standing

Last Man Standing is a league-based competition whereby each player must select a different team in each game week of the league to win in the hope of progressing to the next round/game week. Those participants with winning selections will stay in the competition the next week while others will be knocked out. Your challenge is to be the last person standing and collect the prize money! Competitions offered include the Premier League in England and the League of Ireland.

How to Play

The organiser will choose the league, entry fee and start week for the competition. Once entered, players must choose one team each week and that team must win for them to progress. You cannot choose the same team more than once. In Last Man Standing, the key is knowing when to use your big teams and when to keep them for another day!

Rules - Last Man Standing
• Each participant must select one team each week to win. If your selection fails to win (i.e. draws or loses) you are eliminated.
• You can only choose the same team once (unless the competition progresses beyond the first series of fixtures).
• The deadline for team selection is two hours before the first match of that week.
• If you fail to submit a team you will be assigned the first team in alphabetical order you have not previously selected.
• In a week where domestic cup competitions prevent the entry of some of the league’s teams, there will be no fixtures that week.
• Where a match is postponed/cancelled due to unforeseen circumstances, those who selected the teams involved will progress to the next round.
• Email reminders will be sent out on a weekly basis along with previews. For GDPR purposes you must opt in to receive these reminders.
• Entry fee is €5, €10, €20 or €40, depending on what the game organiser chooses.
• The winner will receive 95% of the pot after fees are deducted.
• If the competition is still running at the end of the season, it will continue from the start of the following season or, on proposal by the game organiser and following agreement by the remaining players, continue to another tournament.
• If all remaining players lose, they all go through to the next round. If some of the remaining players draw and others lose, only the teams that drew progress. If all remaining players draw, they all progress to the next round.

Prediction Competitions

Prediction Competitions give participants to chance to pit their wits against friends and colleagues predicting the correct scores and/or results of that particular tournament! Competitions offered include the FIFA World Cup in football, the Cheltenham racing festival and all four of the golf majors.

How to Play

The organiser selects the competition and how the prize money will be distributed. Participants can then join by following the link or code given to them by the organisers. The tournament fixtures/festival schedule will be available to all participants upon entry, where they can select their predicted outcome for each match/race. Some research here might help to give you the edge!

Rules - Prediction Competitions
• The organiser sets the entry fee (€5, €10, €20 or €50).
• In football tournament “result predictor” competitions, there is 1 point awarded for a correct result and 2 points for the correct score.
• In horse racing predictor competitions, there are 2 points awarded for a jockey win and 1 point for a trainer win.
• Your entry will be confirmed upon receipt of payment.
• Once all players are entered, the draw will take place.
• The prize money distributed will be 95% of the pot after fees are deducted.
